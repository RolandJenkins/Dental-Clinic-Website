Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our dental services and practice.

General Questions

How often should I visit the dentist?

We recommend visiting the dentist every six months for a routine checkup and cleaning. However, some patients may need more frequent visits depending on their oral health needs.

Do you accept dental insurance?

Yes, we accept most traditional dental insurance plans. Please contact our office to verify that we accept your specific plan.

What payment options do you offer?

We accept Visa, Mastercard, Discover, American Express, CareCredit, cash, and personal checks. We also offer financing options through CareCredit with No Interest financing or low minimum monthly payments. Visit CareCredit.com to learn more or apply online.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring your insurance card, a valid photo ID, and any completed patient forms. If you haven't filled out the forms in advance, plan to arrive 15 minutes early. You can download and print the forms from our Patient Forms page.

Appointment Questions

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment by calling our office at (715) 627-4391 or by using our online Book an Appointment form.

What is your cancellation policy?

We understand that schedules change. If you need to cancel or reschedule your appointment, please call us as soon as possible. We request at least 24 hours notice to allow us to offer your time slot to another patient who may be waiting.

Do you offer emergency dental services?

Yes, we provide emergency services for our existing patients after hours. If you're experiencing a dental emergency, please call our office at (715) 627-4391. If you're a new patient with an emergency, we will do our best to accommodate you.

Treatment Questions

Is teeth whitening safe?

Yes, professional teeth whitening is safe and effective when performed or supervised by a dentist. We offer professional whitening options that are much safer and more effective than over-the-counter products. We'll evaluate your teeth and gums to ensure you're a good candidate for whitening.

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care and maintenance, dental implants can last a lifetime. They have a success rate of over 95% and are designed to be a permanent solution for missing teeth.

Are dental X-rays safe?

Yes, dental X-rays are very safe. The amount of radiation exposure from dental X-rays is minimal and the benefits of detecting dental problems early far outweigh any minimal risks.

Do you offer sedation dentistry?

Yes, we offer nitrous oxide (laughing gas) to help patients feel more comfortable during dental procedures. This is especially helpful for patients with dental anxiety or those undergoing longer procedures.

Children's Dental Care

At what age should my child first visit the dentist?

We recommend that children have their first dental visit by their first birthday or within six months of their first tooth appearing.

Do you treat children?

Yes, we welcome patients of all ages, including children. Dr. O'Brien and our team are experienced in working with young patients and strive to make their visits comfortable and positive.
